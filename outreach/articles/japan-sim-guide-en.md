# Best SIM Card for Foreigners in Japan (2026)

Most "best SIM in Japan" articles are written for people who already live here comfortably.

That is not the hard part.

The hard part is week one:

- you may not have a Japanese credit card yet
- you may not want to do everything in Japanese
- you may need eSIM immediately after landing
- you may not know which documents are actually required

That is why I built **JapanSIMGuide**, a multilingual guide focused on the questions foreigners and new residents actually ask:

- Which carrier is easiest to sign up for?
- Can I use a foreign card?
- Is eSIM available right away?
- How much more expensive are docomo, au, and SoftBank?
- When is Rakuten *not* the right answer?

Full guide and calculator: https://tetsu363636.github.io/japan-sim-guide/?src=article

## The quick version

For many new residents, Rakuten Mobile is the simplest starting point because it combines:

- online signup
- eSIM activation
- English support
- foreign card acceptance
- lower monthly cost than Japan's Big 3 carriers

At around the 20GB level, the guide compares Rakuten at **JPY 2,178/month** with the larger-carrier range of roughly **JPY 4,565-7,425/month**, depending on carrier and plan.

That gap matters more than it looks. A family switching away from a Big 3 plan can save on the order of **JPY 150,000-230,000 per year** at comparable usage tiers.

## Why the comparison is different from typical Japan mobile roundups

Many comparison posts focus on raw coverage maps or the lowest possible teaser price.

That misses the friction foreigners hit first.

The guide gives extra weight to:

1. whether you can sign up without a Japanese credit card
2. whether you can finish the process online
3. whether the flow works well in English
4. whether your residence card and temporary setup are enough to get moving
5. whether the plan still makes sense after the promo copy is stripped away

In other words, it is not just "Which carrier is cheapest?" It is "Which carrier is least painful for a newcomer to Japan?"

## Where Rakuten is not the best fit

I work at Rakuten, so this matters to say clearly.

Rakuten is not the cleanest answer for everyone.

If you live in a rural area, spend a lot of time in mountains, or want the brand reassurance of docomo / au / SoftBank no matter the cost, you may prefer a different carrier.

If you use almost no data every month, a low-usage alternative can also be a better fit.

The point of the guide is not to pretend those tradeoffs do not exist. It is to make them obvious so newcomers can decide faster and with fewer surprises.
