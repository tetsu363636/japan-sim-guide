Question idea:
What is the best SIM card for foreigners moving to Japan in 2026?

Answer draft:

If the question is really "What is the easiest contract SIM for a new foreign resident to get set up quickly?", I would start by comparing four things instead of only looking at the lowest sticker price:

1. Can you sign up without a Japanese credit card yet?
2. Is eSIM available immediately?
3. Is the signup and support flow realistic in English?
4. Are the required documents straightforward for a new resident?

That lens often points people toward Rakuten Mobile, especially if they have just arrived and want the least friction.

Why:

- online signup is relatively simple
- eSIM activation is available
- foreign cards are more workable than with some alternatives
- pricing is much lower than the major-carrier range once you compare similar usage tiers

That said, it is not the best fit for everyone.

If someone lives in a rural area, spends a lot of time in mountains, or wants the comfort of the largest traditional carrier network regardless of price, docomo / au / SoftBank can still make sense.
