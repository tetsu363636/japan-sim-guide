Trust notes

Methodology, editorial notes, and disclosure

This page explains how JapanSIMGuide is maintained, what gets compared, which newcomer-specific criteria matter most, and where the author has incentives that should be obvious to readers.

Last updated July 22, 2026

Why this page exists

JapanSIMGuide makes a strong recommendation, includes an employee referral link, and compares plans that change over time. That creates a responsibility to explain the decision rules behind the guide, not just the conclusion.

If you are evaluating whether to trust the main guide, this page is the short answer to "How did you decide that?"

How plans are compared

  • Prices are normalized to tax-included monthly cost wherever possible.
  • Comparison tables prioritize common newcomer decision points: around 3GB, around 20GB, and unlimited usage.
  • Features only matter if they change the real signup experience: eSIM availability, card acceptance, English support, and whether a store visit is likely.
  • When a carrier offers many discount combinations, the guide favors the simplest publicly visible comparison instead of an optimistic stack that many newcomers cannot use on day one.

Foreigner-specific criteria

  • Can someone sign up without a Japanese credit card yet?
  • Is a Japanese-language-only support flow likely to block the user?
  • Can the SIM be activated quickly after arrival, especially by eSIM?
  • Do identity documents commonly held by mid- or long-stay residents work in the standard application flow?
  • Would a tourist visa user need a different category entirely, such as prepaid or visitor eSIM?

What gets updated

  • Headline pricing and data-tier comparison tables.
  • Referral bonus copy, because campaign terms can move.
  • Signup requirements and FAQ items that affect new residents directly.
  • Coverage and speed commentary when the guide needs clearer caveats.
  • Language navigation, hreflang, and structured data so every language page remains discoverable and internally consistent.

Important limits

  • This guide is not an official Rakuten page and is not endorsed by Rakuten Group, Inc.
  • Carrier pricing, campaign bonuses, and signup rules can change after publication.
  • Coverage summaries are directional and should not replace checking a specific address before signup.
  • No single plan is best for every user; rural-heavy use and ultra-low-data use cases can point elsewhere.

Source categories reviewed for each refresh

Carrier materials

Plan pages, pricing pages, campaign notes, signup requirement pages, and coverage-map references for the carriers discussed in the guide.

Product-experience notes

The guide also reflects the author's personal signup and usage experience, especially where official copy does not explain newcomer friction very well.

Site maintenance checks

Canonical URLs, hreflang links, structured data, social preview assets, and multilingual navigation are reviewed because these affect discovery and sharing, not just reader experience.

Disclosure

The author works at Rakuten Group and the main guide contains an official employee referral link for Rakuten Mobile. The author receives an internal incentive when someone signs up through that link.

Because of that conflict, the guide intentionally includes sections about who should not use Rakuten Mobile, where rural coverage can still be weaker, and why very low-usage users may prefer another option. The goal is not to hide the incentive; it is to make it explicit and then give readers enough information to judge the recommendation anyway.

If something looks outdated

The safest rule is still the simplest one: verify the current pricing and eligibility details on each carrier's official site before you apply. The guide is designed to narrow the decision quickly, not replace the final source of truth.
